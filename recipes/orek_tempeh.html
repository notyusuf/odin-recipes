Orek Tempeh

Description:

Orek tempeh is an Indonesian dish made with tempeh, a fermented soybean product. It is a stir-fry dish that is typically made with onions, garlic, chili peppers, kecap manis (sweet soy sauce), tamarind paste, palm sugar, salt, and pepper. Orek tempeh can be served over rice or noodles.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of tempeh, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 red chili pepper, thinly sliced (optional)/li>
  • 1 tablespoon kecap manis (sweet soy sauce)/li>
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind past/li>
  • 1 teaspoon palm suga/li>
  • 1/2 teaspoon sal/li>
  • 1/4 teaspoon black peppe/li>
  • Vegetable oil, for fryin/li>

Steps:

  1. Heat some vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the tempeh and cook, stirring occasionally, until golden brown and crispy. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  2. Add the onion and garlic to the pan and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened. Add the chili pepper, if using, and cook for 1 minute more.
  3. Add the kecap manis, tamarind paste, palm sugar, salt, and pepper to the pan and stir to combine. Bring to a simmer and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened slightly.
  4. Return the tempeh to the pan and stir to coat in the sauce. Cook for 1-2 minutes more, or until heated through.
  5. Serve immediately over rice or noodles.

Tips:

  • For a spicier dish, add more chili peppers.
  • For a more flavorful dish, marinate the tempeh in the kecap manis, tamarind paste, palm sugar, salt, and pepper for at least 30 minutes before cooking.
  • If you don't have tamarind paste, you can substitute 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or vinegar.
  • You can also add other vegetables to the dish, such as carrots, bell peppers, or mushrooms.
